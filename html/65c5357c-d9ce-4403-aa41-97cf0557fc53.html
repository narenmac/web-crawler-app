The way we build and scale AI-powered applications is changing faster than anyone could have imagined. Over the last couple of years, we’ve seen dramatic shifts in how developers design experiences and solve challenges with large language models (LLMs). But as trends continue to evolve—often faster than we can adopt them—the big question becomes: How do you find an application platform that doesn’t just keep up but is truly future-proof? 

Whether you’re scaling retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), adopting function calling, or exploring agentic architectures, the Tanzu Platform with Tanzu AI Solutions is here to help you adapt and thrive in every stage of your app’s evolution.

From 2023 to Today: Retrieval-Augmented Apps at Scale 

This year became a tipping point for retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) applications. Developers and organizations alike fully embraced RAG as a valuable use case for maximizing ROI for models and by extension GenAI apps. RAG applications tap into external data sources like knowledge bases and internal databases to provide more accurate, context-aware, and up-to-date responses. By embedding retrieval right into the pipeline, RAG broke through the barriers of static models, enabling more dynamic and personalized app experiences with more accurate responses.

The Rise of Function Calling in 2024 

Just as the industry settled into RAG-dominance, another seismic shift was happening in 2024: function calling. Suddenly, developers could couple LLMs with external APIs to seamlessly execute real-world tasks. This innovation turned static interactions into actionable workflows. 

Imagine an app that not only generates travel itineraries but also books flights and hotels in the same flow. Or a customer service bot that doesn’t just parse an issue but fires off a command to reset your internet connection in real time. These weren’t just possibilities anymore—they became the new expectations for apps with practical functionality. 

Function calling created dev challenges, too. Extending your infrastructure to incorporate API orchestration required a platform that could ensure reliability, security, and scalability as the number of external integrations grew. Once again, the Tanzu Platform can provide value in this scenario by helping developers tame the complexity while staying agile enough to integrate new and evolving APIs effortlessly.

Looking Ahead to 2025: The Year of Agentic App Architectures 

Prepare for another shift because 2025 will be the year of agent-driven apps. What are agents, and why are they such a big deal? 

While agentic app patterns can be inclusive of RAG and function calling, agentic apps can be so much more. Unlike static LLM-driven apps that simply respond to predetermined inputs, agents take initiative, proactively discovering and exploring solutions to dynamic problems. Fueled by powerful LLMs, agents analyze user intent, determine tasks to execute, and work autonomously (or collaboratively with other agents) to deliver highly personalized and intelligent experiences. 

Picture this scenario:

  • A supply-chain management AI agent that forecasts disruptions and automatically retools logistics to accommodate them.
  • An insurance platform AI agent that can autonomously analyze claims, assess market conditions, and negotiate with customers while keeping adjusters in the loop.

It’s not just AI answering questions anymore—it’s AI thinking in workflows, orchestrating solutions that previously required hands-on human interaction.

But agentic architectures don’t come without their roadblocks. They demand deeply adaptable infrastructure, new security paradigms, dynamic scalability as well as robust and automated testing and performance analytics. A truly future-proof app platform like Tanzu Platform, which already includes Tanzu AI Solutions today, equips developers, IT teams, and leaders to support these advanced workloads while continuing to iterate at speed.
